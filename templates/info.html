Information Page

"For Students By Students"

The Problem

Depression in teens has also been proven to be a crucial factor in a student's academic performance and concentration. Additionally, in New York, six out of every 100,000 15-19 year olds die due to self harm. Even though this is not as many teens dying as there are in the US as a whole, every life matters. A few causes of the recent increase in these numbers are lack of knowledge on teen mental health and difficulty to access professional help. Around 45% of all people in the US consider themselves to know about mental health, this means there are plenty of others who still have more to learn. There are available resources to inform oneself on mental health issues as well as resources in school for students. However, these resources may not be able to help as much as listening to a professional or someone else's firsthand experience would be able to.

Our Solution

We noticed this unfilled niche and decided to make a website to fill it. Our website has many features that are not present in other mental health websites, such as a chat room that allows students to speak with other students having the same issues. In addition, we will soon have a way for students to contact a professional, making it easier to contact someone who is knowledgeable about thier specific problem.
