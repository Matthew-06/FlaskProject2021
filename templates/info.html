Information Page

This website is for spreading awareness on Mental Heath and helping students who have issues to get help.

The Problem

Many people today tend to ignore the present mental health issues among students in New York City. In 2019, when the COVID-19 pandemic started, depression among teens from the ages of 15-19 spiked by an overwhelming 35.9%. Over the past year with online school and Zoom classes, one can only imagine how much this percentage has increased. Depression in teens has also been proven to be a crucial factor in a student's academic performance and concentration. Additionally, in New York there are six deaths per every 100,000 people in the same age group of 15-19 caused by self harm. Even though this is not as many teens dying as there are in the US as a whole, every life matters. A few causes of the recent increase in these numbers are lack of knowledge on teen mental health and difficulty to access professional help. Around 45% of all people in the US consider themselves to know about mental health, this means there are plenty of others who still have more to learn. There are available resources to inform oneself on mental health issues as well as resources in school for students. However, these resources may not be able to help as much as a professional would be able to.
